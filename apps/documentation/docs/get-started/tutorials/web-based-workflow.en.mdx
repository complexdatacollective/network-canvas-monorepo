<SummarySection>

This tutorial describes the web-based protocol and data workflow for Network Canvas, built around [Architect Web](/en/design-protocols/getting-started) and [Fresco](/en/collect-data/fresco/about). It is the browser-based counterpart to the desktop [Protocol and Data Workflows](/en/get-started/tutorials/protocol-and-data-workflows) tutorial.

</SummarySection>
<PrerequisitesSection>

To follow along, you should have a deployed Fresco instance to run your study. Fresco is self-hosted, so you (or your institution's IT department) will need to [deploy it](/en/collect-data/fresco/deployment/guide) first, or you can explore the [sandbox](/en/collect-data/fresco/sandbox) to try it without deploying.

</PrerequisitesSection>
</SummaryCard>

## Introduction

The desktop workflows built around [Interviewer](/en/collect-data/interviewer) require you to deploy a protocol file to every interview device,
and to collect a separate data export back from each one.

The **web-based workflow** removes both of those steps. You author your protocol in Architect Web, upload it once to your Fresco dashboard, and participants complete interviews in their browser. There is no software to install on interview devices, and protocol deployment and data export are both handled centrally through the dashboard.

## Step 1: Build the protocol

Design your protocol in [Architect Web](https://architect.networkcanvas.com), the browser-based version of the Architect protocol designer. There is nothing to install, and the protocol-building steps are shared with Architect Desktop. When your protocol is ready, use **Download** to export it as a `.netcanvas` file.

See [Getting started with Architect Web](/en/design-protocols/getting-started) for the full walkthrough.

## Step 2: Deploy the protocol

Upload the `.netcanvas` file once to your Fresco dashboard, instead of copying it to each device. Fresco validates the protocol, uploads any assets it contains, and makes it available to participants.

1. Open your Fresco instance and log in to the dashboard.
2. Navigate to the **protocols** page and click **Upload protocol**.
3. Select the `.netcanvas` file you exported from Architect Web, then click **Upload**.

See [Using Fresco — Uploading protocols](/en/collect-data/fresco/using-fresco#uploading-protocols) for full details.

## Step 3: Recruit participants and conduct interviews

Participants complete the interview **in a web browser** by visiting a participation URL — there is no app to install. They can use any computer or tablet with a [supported browser](/en/collect-data/fresco/faq#which-browsers-are-supported); smartphones and small-screen devices are not supported.

Fresco supports two recruitment strategies, which can be combined: adding **known participants** (individually or via CSV import) and sharing a unique URL with each, or enabling **anonymous recruitment** so participants self-enroll via a single shared URL. For the full steps — adding participants, generating URLs, and monitoring progress — see [Using Fresco](/en/collect-data/fresco/using-fresco#participants).

## Step 4: Export the data

Interview data is stored centrally in Fresco's database, so there is no per-device export to manage. Once participants have completed interviews, export the data from the dashboard.

1. Navigate to the **interviews** page.
2. Either select specific interviews and click **Export selected**, or click **Export Interview Data** to export all (or all unexported) interviews.
3. Confirm the file types and export options, then click **Start export process**.

You can export to **CSV** or **GraphML**, exactly as in the desktop workflow. For full details, see [Using Fresco — Exporting data](/en/collect-data/fresco/using-fresco#exporting-data) and the [Data Export guide](/en/analyze-data/data-export).

<TipBox>

Because Fresco is self-hosted, your data security, backup, and retention posture depends on how and where your instance is deployed. Export your data regularly rather than treating the live database as your only copy, and review the [IT FAQ](/en/collect-data/fresco/deployment/it-faq) with whoever maintains your deployment.

</TipBox>
