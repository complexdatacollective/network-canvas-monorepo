Before you build a protocol or run an interview, it helps to plan how your study
will move through the Network Canvas tools from end to end. Whichever tools you
choose, the shape of a study is the same: **design** a protocol, **collect** data
by running it, and **export** that data for **analysis**.

## Workflows at a glance

The diagram below summarises the end-to-end flow and the three main ways to run a
study. Which one fits depends on whether your interviews happen in person or
remotely, and whether your interview devices have an internet connection.

<ImageFullWidth
  src="/assets/img/offline-data/workflows-overview.png"
  alt="Diagram of the Network Canvas workflow: design a protocol, collect data, and export for analysis, with desktop online, desktop offline, and web-based branches."
/>

- **Desktop, online** — build a protocol, deploy it to the [Interviewer](/en/collect-data/interviewer) app on each device via a cloud file service, and export data back through the cloud.
- **Desktop, offline** — the same desktop flow, but protocols and data move on a USB drive when devices have no internet connection.
- **Web-based** — author in [Architect Web](/en/design-protocols/getting-started), upload your protocol once to a [Fresco](/en/collect-data/fresco/about) dashboard, and let participants complete interviews in their browser, with all data stored and exported centrally.

For the full step-by-step walkthrough of each option — including protocol
deployment, data export, and backup best practices — see the
[Protocol and Data Workflows](/en/collect-data/protocol-and-data-workflows)
tutorial.

## Before you start collecting data

Planning a study is more than choosing a workflow. Two topics are worth reviewing
early, because they shape how you design your protocol and handle participant data:

- **[GDPR Compliance Guide](/en/get-started/planning-a-study/gdpr-compliance)** — how Interviewer and Fresco handle participant data and what your responsibilities are.
- **[IRB and Security Best Practices](/en/get-started/planning-a-study/irb-best-practices)** — how to think about data security and institutional review when working with sensitive data.
