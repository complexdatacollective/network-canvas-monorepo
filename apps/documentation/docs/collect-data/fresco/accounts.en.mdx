Fresco protects the researcher dashboard with one or more accounts. This guide covers signing in, adding team members, and
securing accounts with passkeys and two-factor authentication.

<TipBox>

Multiple accounts, passkeys, and two-factor authentication require **Fresco v4 or later**.
Earlier versions supported only a single password-protected account.

</TipBox>

## Signing in

Sign in from the dashboard login screen with your username and password (or passkey). If
two-factor authentication is enabled, you will then be prompted for a 6-digit code from
your authenticator app (or a recovery code). Repeated failed attempts are rate-limited.

## Changing your password

To change your password, open **Settings → User Management → Change Password**, enter your
current password, then set and confirm a new one. New passwords must be at least 8
characters and include a lowercase letter, an uppercase letter, a number, and a symbol.

If your account uses a passkey instead of a password, there is no password to change.

## Adding team members

<TipBox danger>

**All Fresco accounts are equal.** There are no roles or permission tiers: every account
is a full administrator with complete access to all data, and can create or delete other
accounts. Only create accounts for people you trust with the entire study.

</TipBox>

Give each person their own account rather than sharing credentials. Open **Settings → User
Management**, click **Add User**, and set a username and initial password (the new user can
switch to a passkey afterwards). Select accounts and delete them to remove users.

You cannot delete your own account while signed in to it, and at least one account must
always remain.

## Passkeys

A passkey lets you sign in using your device's biometrics (Touch ID, Windows Hello) or a
hardware security key instead of a password. It can be an account's primary sign-in method
or a second factor alongside a password.

From **Settings**, use **Add passkey** to register one (you can register more than one and
name each), or **Remove** to delete one. An account can also **Switch to Passkey
Authentication** or **Switch to Password Authentication** at any time.

## Two-factor authentication

Two-factor authentication adds a one-time code on top of your password. Fresco uses standard
TOTP, compatible with apps such as Google Authenticator, 1Password, and Authy.

Open **Settings → Two-Factor Authentication**, scan the QR code into your authenticator app,
and enter the current code to confirm. You will then be asked for a code each time you sign
in. When you enable it, Fresco gives you single-use **recovery codes** to sign in if you
lose your authenticator device.

<TipBox danger>

Store your recovery codes somewhere safe and separate from your authenticator device, such
as an institutional password manager. You can regenerate a fresh set at any time, which
invalidates the previous codes.

</TipBox>

## Recovering access

Fresco has **no email-based "forgot password" flow**. You can only [change your
password](#changing-your-password) while signed in. If you are locked out, your options, in
order of preference, are:

1. **Use a recovery code** to sign in (if two-factor authentication was enabled).
2. **Ask another administrator** to **Reset Auth** for your account from User Management.
   This clears your existing passkeys and two-factor authentication and gives the
   administrator a temporary password to pass to you, which you use to sign back in and set
   up new credentials.
3. As a last resort, if every account is inaccessible and no recovery codes remain,
   [reset your database](/en/collect-data/fresco/deployment/troubleshooting#instance-expired---reset-your-database).
   **This destroys all data and unexported interviews.**

Because of this, keep your credentials and recovery codes somewhere safe and enrol more than
one administrator, so a second person can always restore access.

For how authentication works at the infrastructure level (session cookies, encryption, rate
limiting), see the [FAQ for IT Departments](/en/collect-data/fresco/deployment/it-faq).
